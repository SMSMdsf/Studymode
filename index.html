Then the other gull flew up, wheeled around and landed and picked something up. Some awkward, heavy thing. The gull flew low and slowly, dragging the heavy thing. Finally, the gull dropped it beside the eye. This new thing had five legs. It moved. The gulls thought it was a strange kind of crab. They thought they had found a strange crab and a strange clam.

They did not know they had found the Iron Man's eye and the Iron Man's right hand.

But as soon as the eye and the hand got together the eye looked at the hand. Its light glowed blue. The hand stood up on three fingers and its thumb, and craned its forefinger like a long nose. It felt around. It touched the eye. Gleefully it picked up the eye, and tucked it under its middle finger. The eye peered out, between the forefinger and thumb. Now the hand could see.

Key moment: The Iron Man's parts beginning to reunite and function together.

It looked around. Then it darted and jabbed one of the gulls with its stiffly held finger, then darted at the other and jabbed him. The two gulls danced this way and that, squawking and struggling, and then they both took to the air.

Algebra - Solving Equations

Today's Problem

Problem 1: Linear Equations

Solve for x:

2x + 5 = 17

Solution: Subtract 5 from both sides: 2x = 12

Divide by 2: x = 6

Problem 2: Quadratic Equations

Solve for x:

x² - 5x + 6 = 0

Solution: Factor: (x - 2)(x - 3) = 0

x = 2 or x = 3

Problem 3: Systems of Equations

Solve:

x + y = 5
x - y = 1

Solution: Add equations: 2x = 6, so x = 3

Substitute: 3 + y = 5, so y = 2

Physics - Motion and Forces

Today's Lesson

Newton's First Law of Motion

An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion unless acted upon by an external force. This is the law of inertia. For example, when you stop a car suddenly, your body tends to move forward because it wants to maintain its state of motion.

Newton's Second Law of Motion

The acceleration of an object depends on the force applied to it and its mass. This is expressed as F = ma, where F is force, m is mass, and a is acceleration. The greater the force applied, the greater the acceleration. The greater the mass, the less acceleration for the same force.

Newton's Third Law of Motion

For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. When you jump, you push down on the ground with a force, and the ground pushes up on you with an equal force, propelling you upward. This law is fundamental to understanding how forces work in pairs.

Practical Application: Friction

Friction is a force that opposes motion between two surfaces in contact. It acts in the direction opposite to the motion. There are three types: static friction (prevents motion), kinetic friction (during motion), and rolling friction (for rolling objects). Understanding friction is crucial in engineering and everyday life.
